# Community code of conduct

OpenAI4S welcomes contributors across research disciplines, institutions,
experience levels, identities, and backgrounds. Participation is a privilege
conditioned on making the project safe, rigorous, and constructive.

## Expected conduct

- Discuss ideas and evidence, not a person's worth, identity, fluency, title,
  institution, location, or access to compute.
- Give specific, actionable review feedback and accept correction without
  retaliation. Credit prior work and collaborators accurately.
- Protect credentials, private correspondence, personal data, unpublished
  research, embargoed results, and responsible-disclosure material.
- Be transparent about synthetic data, model-generated content, conflicts of
  interest, limitations, and work you did not independently verify.
- Respect scientific, software, safety, biosecurity, export-control, and
  platform rules that apply to the work.

Harassment, threats, stalking, sexualized attention, discriminatory language,
doxxing, impersonation, plagiarism, fabrication, coercion, sustained
disruption, and publishing another person's private information are not
acceptable. Technical disagreement, a rejected change, or a request for
evidence is not by itself misconduct when expressed professionally.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, discussions, pull requests, reviews,
commits, project chat, events, and private communications undertaken as a
project representative. It also applies when use of the project name or access
obtained through the project creates a reasonable connection to the community.

## Reporting and enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to
[minecraftzhanggongbo@outlook.com](mailto:minecraftzhanggongbo@outlook.com).
Do not put sensitive evidence in a public issue. Include only the information
needed to understand the incident; maintainers may request more through a
private channel. A maintainer named in or closely connected to a report must
recuse themselves.

Maintainers will protect reporter and witness privacy to the extent practical,
consider context and patterns of behavior, and prohibit retaliation. Depending
on severity and history, responses may include a private correction, formal
warning, content removal, temporary participation limits, or a permanent ban.
Immediate protective action may precede a full review when safety requires it.
Knowingly false or retaliatory reports are themselves violations; an
unsubstantiated good-faith report is not.
